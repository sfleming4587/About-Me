Software Optimization for a 16-bit DAC

As part of a year-long senior design project sponsored by Fieldline Medical, I served as the Software Lead on a team tasked with designing a 16-bit Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) from scratch. With no prior experience, I self-taught SystemVerilog, FPGA development, and Quartus Prime in a short timeframe, delivering a high-performance solution under tight constraints. Below are key highlights of my contributions, showcasing my skills in optimization, signal processing, and hardware-software integration.

Oversampling Pipeline

One of my primary responsibilities was designing an oversampler to transition signals from a 250 kHz input to a 16 MHz binary output, a critical step for the DAC’s performance. I developed the oversampling_pipeline.sv module to handle this clock domain crossing efficiently. Initially, the 16-bit multiplication and division operations caused resource bottlenecks. To address this, I implemented a pipelining strategy, delaying the response by one clock cycle but significantly reducing resource usage. This optimization improved the DAC’s throughput and demonstrated my ability to balance trade-offs in real-time systems.

Delta Sigma Modulation

Another key contribution was implementing Delta Sigma Modulation in the delta_sigma_single.sv file. This module converted a 16-bit digital input into a high-frequency pulse density output of 1s and 0s, achieving an analog output (Thanks to our hardware team!) with precision down to 1/(2^16) between -3.3 and 3.3 volts. Mastering this complex signal processing technique required deep dives into modulation theory and SystemVerilog, showcasing my ability to learn and apply advanced concepts to meet stringent accuracy requirements.
